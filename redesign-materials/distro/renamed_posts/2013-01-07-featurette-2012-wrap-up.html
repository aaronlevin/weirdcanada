2012 has come and gone, and once again we’ve decided to join the fray of year-end retrospectives sans stocking-stuffer advertorial.

2012 Albums We Missed

  • Femminielli - Double Invitation ("I blame the fact that the LP is not yet grippable in North America." - Jesse Locke)
  • Sam Coffey and the Iron Lungs - Shin No Skin ("If we’d tried to get close enough to review this one, it’d probably have resulted in us getting chased off some deranged punk farm with shotguns and pitchforks." - Max Cotter)
  • You'll Never Get To Heaven - You'll Never Get To Heaven ("There's no excuse for missing this. It's been glued to my turntable for months, and I simply haven't found the words." - Jesse Locke)
  • Maica Mia - Sparcity Blues ("Montréalaise siren Maica Mia's Sparcity Blues is a near perfect album. She and sideman Johnny Paradise have produced a hauntingly honest LP that begs one to both laugh and cry. Raw, human and unafraid of experimentation this is the folk music the end times beget and it is joyous. Beautifully packaged to boot." - James Goddard)
  • Teledrome - Dial Tone 7" ("How did this happen???" - Paul Lawton)
  • Northern Haze - Northern Haze ("How could we miss a reissue of vintage Inuit hard-rock from an Igloolik band that was originally released by the CBC?" - Aaron Levin)
  • The Soupcans - Good Feelings (James Lindsay)
  • Phil Console - The Modern Fog (Benoit Poirier)
  • Neil Young - Psychedelic Pill (Graeme Langdon)

Pour clore l'année 2012, nous avons décidé une fois de plus de contribuer à la cuvée annuelle de rétrospectives de toutes sortes. Les listes Top 10 qui polluent le paysage médiatique de décembre étant souvent ennuyantes, aliénantes et/ou péniblement publireportagesques, nous avons choisi d'attendre que l'année soit terminée et de promouvoir la curiosité et les découvertes plutôt que de chercher le « meilleur » de tout. Sans plus tarder, voici notre rétrospective 2012 !
